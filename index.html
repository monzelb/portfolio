About me

I am a software developer who loves to create, collaborate, and produce meaningful work. I am inspired by great ideas, user-centric design, and efficient code, and prioritize these in my work.

My time as a Buddhist monk shaped much of my perspective on life, work, and relationships. I aspire to build a compassionate, inclusive culture wherever I work.

I am a believer that the power of technology can be leveraged to create a better world.

Milestones

Ohio State University/ University of Cincinnati

My desire to study the nature of the universe and the human mind led me to study Psychology at OSU, and later, Philosophy at UC.

2008-2014

Buddhist monk

From an early age, Buddhism spoke to me in ways that nothing else had, and in my twenties I devoted several years to living and practicing what was most meaningful to me. I'm a much kinder, happier person as a result!

I helped launch this sustainable food-tech startup which serves gourmet plant-based meals in reusable containers. Being involved in a startup from its beginning taught me a lot about business operations, and peaked my interest in tech.

2015

Web development

After being exposed to the bustling tech scene in San Francisco, I decided I needed to learn to code. With the help of online courses such as Free Code Camp, Thinkful, and Codecademy, I began building my earliest projects.

2016

Non-profit management

Against the Stream is an internationally recognized non-profit meditation community which offers classes in meditation, mindfulness, and addiction recovery. I had the good fortune to serve as the manager of their center in San Francisco, where I managed web content (Wordpress), email marketing campaigns (Constant Contact), social media, accounting, and community organizing.

2017

Bootcamp

After learning software development on my own for a year and a half I realized that I wanted to plunge in deeper and signed up for General Assembly's 12 week web development bootcamp, where I learned full-stack development in Javascript and Ruby on Rails, and developed the skill of picking up languages and frameworks very rapidly.

2017

Berkeley Public Schools Fund

I am working with with a team of developers building a crowd-funding app for Berkeley Public Schools, which allows teachers apply for grants for their classes, which anyone can donate to through the app.
